Apr 24, 2026 Why we created Loophole The Loophole Team ›

Loophole started with a simple frustration — we kept running into walls that didn't need to be there. Existing tools were either too rigid, too bloated, or just not built with real workflows in mind. We wanted something that got out of the way and let you actually do the work.

So we built it ourselves. Not because it was easy, but because we were tired of waiting for someone else to. Loophole is our answer to the question: what would this look like if it were built right, from the ground up, with no legacy baggage?

This post is the beginning of us being more open about what we're doing, why we made certain calls, and where we're headed next. We'll keep writing. Hope you keep reading.
